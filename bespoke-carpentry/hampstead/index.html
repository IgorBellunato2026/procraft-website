Bespoke Carpentry, In Hampstead

Joinery for a Hampstead house
is detail work.

Hampstead’s housing stock is unusually varied: Georgian village houses around Church Row and Flask Walk, Victorian terraces dropping down to Belsize Park, Arts and Crafts homes around the Heath, and Edwardian mansion blocks lining Fitzjohn’s Avenue and Finchley Road. Each rewards a different kind of attention.

The Hampstead brief tends to be honest and practical: Restore or replace joinery to suit the period of the house — fitted wardrobes for Victorian and Edwardian rooms, panelling and library walls for Arts and Crafts homes around the Heath.

This is the work we do. We design and hand-build joinery specifically for Hampstead’s housing stock. Solid timber face frames, dovetailed drawers, hand-cut mortise and tenon where the build calls for it, painted in your specified palette. Installed by the carpenters who built it, not a sub-contracted team.

Hampstead Considerations

The detail that separates joinery
that belongs from joinery that doesn’t.

A joinery project in a Hampstead Arts and Crafts home is a fundamentally different brief from a joinery project in a flat below the heath. The constraints are different, the planning environment is different, and the buildings reward a different kind of attention.

We have refurbished joinery projects across NW3 long enough to know how Hampstead Conservation Area rules apply in practice, how Camden planning officers handle period extensions, and how the major mansion-block managing agents structure approvals. All of it is factored in at quote stage.
