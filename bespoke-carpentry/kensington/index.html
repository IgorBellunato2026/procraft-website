Bespoke Carpentry, In Kensington

Joinery for a Kensington house
is detail work.

The housing stock between Kensington High Street and Holland Park — stucco-fronted Victorian and Edwardian terraces, Edwardian mansion blocks, garden-square houses around Kensington Square and Edwardes Square — was built before electricity reached it. The joinery project brief here lives or dies on respecting that fabric.

The Kensington brief is almost always the same in shape: Restore or replace joinery to suit the period of the house — fitted wardrobes detailed for the proportions of a stucco-fronted reception, panelling that respects the original architecture.

This is the work we do. We design and hand-build joinery specifically for Kensington’s housing stock. Solid timber face frames, dovetailed drawers, hand-cut mortise and tenon where the build calls for it, painted in your specified palette. Installed by the carpenters who built it, not a sub-contracted team.

Kensington Considerations

The detail that separates joinery
that belongs from joinery that doesn’t.

A joinery project in a Kensington stucco-fronted terrace is not the same brief as a joinery project in a new-build apartment. The variables that matter are different, the constraints are different, and the design moves that work elsewhere fail here.

We have refurbished joinery projects across W8 and SW7 long enough to know what those variables are: where the load-bearing walls live, how Royal Borough of Kensington and Chelsea conservation areas affect external work, how mansion-block managing agents handle freeholder approvals, and where access genuinely constrains delivery. All of it is factored in at quote stage, not discovered mid-project.
