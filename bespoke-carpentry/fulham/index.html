Bespoke Carpentry, In Fulham

Joinery for a Fulham house
is detail work.

Fulham’s housing stock is unusually consistent: Victorian terraces between Fulham Road and Wandsworth Bridge Road, period mansion blocks along New King’s Road, and the garden flats sitting under them. Most ground floors are now opened up across a side-return extension — which is where the joinery project brief gets interesting.

The Fulham brief is almost always the same in shape: Fitted wardrobes, alcove built-ins and panelling for Victorian terraces, often as part of a larger side-return refurbishment.

This is the work we do. We design and hand-build joinery specifically for Fulham’s housing stock. Solid timber face frames, dovetailed drawers, hand-cut mortise and tenon where the build calls for it, painted in your specified palette. Installed by the carpenters who built it, not a sub-contracted team.

Fulham Considerations

The detail that separates joinery
that belongs from joinery that doesn’t.

A joinery project in a Fulham Victorian terrace is a deceptively layered brief. Structural engineering for the steel goalpost, party-wall awareness on both neighbours, building control sign-off, planning where conservation status applies, and a joinery project designed to take full advantage of the new opening rather than treating it as an afterthought.

We have refurbished joinery projects across SW6 long enough to know how those variables interact, where they cause friction if ignored, and how to plan around them. Party-wall awards, structural engineering coordination, building control timelines and managing-agent approvals are all built into the programme at quote stage.
