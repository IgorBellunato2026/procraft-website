Bespoke Carpentry, In Chelsea

Joinery for a Chelsea house
is detail work.

The housing stock between Sloane Square and World’s End is unusually consistent: Victorian and Georgian terraces, intimate mews properties, and the mansion blocks that line Cheyne Walk and the Embankment. None of them were designed around a contemporary joinery project, and none of them respond well to a joinery project designed for somewhere else.

The brief in Chelsea is almost always the same in shape, even if the materials differ. Restore or replace joinery to suit the period of the house — fitted wardrobes that read as if always there, alcove built-ins detailed for cornicing height, panelling that respects the original architecture.

This is the work we do. We design and hand-build joinery specifically for Chelsea’s housing stock. Solid timber face frames, dovetailed drawers, hand-cut mortise and tenon where the build calls for it, painted in your specified palette. Installed by the carpenters who built it, not a sub-contracted team.

Chelsea Considerations

The detail that separates joinery
that belongs from joinery that doesn’t.

A joinery project in a Chelsea Victorian terrace is not the same brief as a joinery project in a new-build apartment. The variables that matter are different, the constraints are different, and the assumptions that work elsewhere fail here.

We have refurbished joinery projects across Chelsea for long enough to know what those variables are, where the planning friction lives, and how to design around the constraints rather than fight them. Original ceiling heights, narrow stair access, mews delivery windows, party-wall sensitivities, freeholder rules in mansion blocks — all of it is factored in at quote stage, not discovered mid-project.
