Refurbishment, In Richmond

A full refurbishment in Richmond
has riverside variables to plan around.

Richmond’s housing stock is unusually varied: Georgian and Victorian terraces around Richmond Green, Edwardian houses climbing Richmond Hill, riverside properties along the Thames, and the listed buildings around Petersham and Ham. None of them respond well to a generic main-contractor approach.

The brief in Richmond is rarely cosmetic. Most of our full refurbishments here involve structural alterations, side-return or rear extensions, full re-services and the restoration of original detail in parallel with new bespoke joinery, kitchens and bathrooms. Riverside addresses also bring flood-zone moisture management into the programme from the start.

This is the work we do. We design and run the project end-to-end. The same crew that takes the kitchen apart builds the new one. The same carpenters that strip the panelling restore it. There is one point of contact for the entire programme, one written specification, one schedule, and one final walk-through.

Richmond Considerations

The variables that decide
whether a refurbishment runs.

A full house refurbishment in a Richmond Georgian terrace is not the same brief as a refurbishment elsewhere. Conservation status, listed-building consent, riverside flood-zone rules and the specific access constraints of Richmond’s narrow lanes all factor into how the project gets delivered.

We have refurbished Richmond homes long enough to know how those variables interact, where they cause friction if ignored, and how to plan around them. Conservation officer expectations, flood-zone specification, party-wall sensitivities and access restrictions are all built into the programme at quote stage.
