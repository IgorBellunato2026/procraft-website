Refurbishment, In Hampstead

A full refurbishment in Hampstead
rewards a different kind of attention.

Hampstead’s housing stock is unusually varied: Georgian village houses around Church Row and Flask Walk, Victorian terraces dropping down to Belsize Park, Arts and Crafts homes around the Heath, Edwardian mansion blocks lining Fitzjohn’s Avenue and Finchley Road. Each rewards a different kind of attention. None of them respond well to a generic main-contractor approach.

The brief in Hampstead is rarely cosmetic. Most of our full refurbishments here involve structural alterations, side-return or rear extensions, full re-services and the restoration of original detail in parallel with new bespoke joinery, kitchens and bathrooms. The programme has to be planned around all of it from day one — not improvised when the wall comes down.

This is the work we do. We design and run the project end-to-end. The same crew that takes the kitchen apart builds the new one. The same carpenters that strip the panelling restore it. There is one point of contact for the entire programme, one written specification, one schedule, and one final walk-through.

Hampstead Considerations

The variables that decide
whether a refurbishment runs.

A full house refurbishment in a Hampstead Arts and Crafts home is not the same brief as a refurbishment in a flat below the heath. The constraints are different, the planning environment is different, and the buildings reward a different kind of attention.

We have refurbished Hampstead homes long enough to know how Hampstead Conservation Area rules apply in practice, how Camden planning officers handle period extensions, and how the major mansion-block managing agents structure approvals. All of it is factored in at quote stage and built into the programme.

What Hampstead clients

Every full refurbishment is priced individually. Igor visits the property, assesses scope, structural requirements, conservation considerations and specification level, then provides a detailed written quotation itemised line by line. We do not publish indicative pricing online — the variables on a Hampstead project are too significant for any range to be useful. Standard 20% VAT is shown separately on every quote.
Yes. Side-return extensions, rear extensions, basement excavations and internal structural alterations are part of most full refurbishments in Hampstead. We coordinate structural engineering, building control, party-wall surveying and London Borough of Camden planning where required, and quote those professional fees separately and transparently.
Yes. Most of Hampstead sits within the Hampstead Conservation Area, with a number of listed village houses around Church Row and Flask Walk. We restore period detail rather than stripping it, and liaise with planning consultants where listed-building consent is required for changes affecting historic fabric.
Yes. Arts and Crafts homes around the Heath and mansion-block apartments along Fitzjohn’s Avenue and Finchley Road are routine briefs for us. We coordinate with managing agents on freeholder requirements, working hours, lift protection and access, and plan the programme around them at the survey stage.
