Refurbishment, In Knightsbridge

A full refurbishment in Knightsbridge
is held to a different standard.

The housing stock around Hans Place, Beauchamp Place and Pavilion Road is unusually high in specification and unusually demanding to refurbish: listed Georgian and Victorian townhouses with original cornicing, panelling and sash windows; Edwardian mansion blocks with strict freeholder rules; and the high-spec apartment buildings that line Knightsbridge itself. None of them respond well to a generic main-contractor approach.

The brief in Knightsbridge is rarely cosmetic. Most of our full refurbishments here involve listed-building consent, internal structural alterations, full re-services and the restoration of original detail in parallel with new bespoke joinery, kitchens and bathrooms. The programme has to be planned around all of it from day one — not improvised when the wall comes down.

This is the work we do. We design and run the project end-to-end. The same crew that takes the kitchen apart builds the new one. The same carpenters that strip the panelling restore it. There is one point of contact for the entire programme, one written specification, one schedule, and one final walk-through.

Knightsbridge Considerations

The variables that decide
whether a refurbishment runs.

A full house refurbishment in a Knightsbridge listed townhouse is not the same brief as a refurbishment in a new-build apartment. The constraints are different, the planning environment is different, and the standard the building holds you to is different.

We have refurbished Knightsbridge homes long enough to know how Hans Town and Knightsbridge Conservation Area rules apply in practice, where listed-building consent is non-negotiable, how the major mansion-block managing agents structure approvals, and how to deliver into addresses where porter coordination and lift protection matter as much as the cabinetry. All of it is factored in at quote stage and built into the programme.
