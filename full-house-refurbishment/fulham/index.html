Refurbishment, In Fulham

A full refurbishment in Fulham
is a side-return brief most of the time.

Fulham’s housing stock is unusually consistent: Victorian terraces between Fulham Road and Wandsworth Bridge Road, period mansion blocks along New King’s Road, and the garden flats sitting under them. Most ground floors here are now opened up across a side-return extension — the design move that defines the typical Fulham refurbishment.

The brief in Fulham is rarely cosmetic. Most of our full refurbishments here involve a side-return or rear extension, full re-services, a new kitchen, bathrooms, structural alterations to the rest of the ground floor, and the restoration of original detail in parallel. The programme has to be planned around all of it from day one — not improvised when the wall comes down.

This is the work we do. We design and run the project end-to-end. The same crew that takes the kitchen apart builds the new one. The same carpenters that strip the panelling restore it. There is one point of contact for the entire programme, one written specification, one schedule, and one final walk-through.

Fulham Considerations

The variables that decide
whether a refurbishment runs.

A full house refurbishment in a Fulham Victorian terrace is a deceptively layered brief. Structural engineering for the steel goalpost, party-wall awareness on both neighbours, building control sign-off, planning where conservation status applies, and a kitchen, flooring and bathroom programme that takes full advantage of the new opening rather than treating it as an afterthought.

We have refurbished Fulham terraces long enough to know how those variables interact, where they cause friction if ignored, and how to plan around them. Party-wall awards, structural engineering coordination, building control timelines and managing-agent approvals are all built into the programme at quote stage.
