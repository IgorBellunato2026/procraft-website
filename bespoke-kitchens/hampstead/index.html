Kitchens, In Hampstead

Kitchens for one of London’s
most architecturally varied villages.

Hampstead is unlike most of central London. The housing stock varies more, the streets are narrower, the gradients are steeper, and the village character around Heath Street, Church Row, Flask Walk and Holly Hill is genuinely intact. Refurbishing a kitchen here means working with a quieter, more pastoral set of constraints — but constraints all the same.

The brief in Hampstead changes more by property type than by street. Georgian and Victorian village houses around Hampstead Village proper want kitchens that are sympathetic to original cornicing, fireplaces and panelling. Arts and Crafts homes around Frognal and Redington Road reward design that respects the original detailing — oak, exposed timber, hand-finished metalwork. Belsize Park’s tall Victorian terraces are often the brief for substantial side-return or rear extensions. Edwardian mansion blocks along Fitzjohn’s Avenue have managing agents and acoustic constraints to satisfy.

We have refurbished kitchens across all of it. Cabinetry is hand-built in our workshop. Worktops are templated on site, slab-matched at every joint. Installation is done by the carpenters who built the cabinetry. Access is planned around the village reality — the steep hill gradients, the narrow lanes, the parking constraints — not against it.

Hampstead Considerations

The detail that separates a kitchen
that belongs from one that doesn’t.

A bespoke kitchen in a Hampstead Arts and Crafts home is a fundamentally different brief from a fitted kitchen in a flat below the heath. The constraints are different, the planning environment is different, and the buildings reward a different kind of attention.

We have refurbished kitchens across NW3 long enough to know how the Hampstead Conservation Area rules apply in practice, where listed-building consent is non-negotiable, how to deliver into addresses with steep hill gradients and narrow village lanes, and how London Borough of Camden handles parking permits and skip licences. All of it is factored in at quote stage.

Yes. The Hampstead Conservation Area covers Hampstead Village and much of NW3, with a high concentration of listed buildings around Church Row, Holly Hill, Flask Walk and the Heath. Internal kitchen refurbishment is normally permitted, but listed-building consent applies to any change affecting historic fabric — including chimney breasts, panelling, plasterwork and original windows. We work to those constraints by default.
Hampstead Village has some of the tightest access constraints in north London — narrow lanes, steep hill gradients around Heath Street and Holly Hill, single-vehicle frontages and limited parking. We arrange contractor parking with London Borough of Camden ahead of every project, plan deliveries around the access reality, and factor lift-and-carry constraints into the programme rather than discovering them mid-project.
