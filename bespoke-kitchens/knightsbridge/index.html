Kitchens, In Knightsbridge

Kitchens for one of the
most listed quarters of London.

Knightsbridge is unusual even by Prime Central London standards. The grid of streets around Hans Place, Cadogan Square, Wilton Crescent and Beauchamp Place contains one of the highest concentrations of listed buildings anywhere in the country. The mansion blocks along Sloane Street, Brompton Road and Pavilion Road are equally distinctive — high-spec, often fully serviced, and held to standards by managing agents that match those of a five-star hotel.

The kitchen brief in Knightsbridge has to operate inside both realities. Listed townhouses demand a contractor who can navigate consent applications, work sympathetically alongside original fabric, and design cabinetry that reads as if it has always belonged in the room. Mansion blocks and high-spec apartments demand a contractor who can satisfy a managing agent on tanking, acoustic specification, working hours and lift protection without compromise.

We design and build kitchens for both. Cabinetry is hand-built in our workshop. Worktops are templated on site and slab-matched at every joint. Installation is done by the carpenters who built the cabinetry — not a sub-contracted team learning the building for the first time.

Knightsbridge Considerations

The detail that separates a kitchen
that belongs from one that doesn’t.

A bespoke kitchen in a Knightsbridge listed townhouse is a fundamentally different brief from a kitchen in a new-build flat. The constraints are different, the planning environment is different, and the standard the building holds you to is different.

We have refurbished kitchens across SW1X and SW7 long enough to know how Hans Town and Knightsbridge Conservation Area rules apply in practice, where listed-building consent is non-negotiable, how the major mansion-block managing agents structure approvals, and how to deliver into addresses where porter coordination and lift protection matter as much as the cabinetry. All of it is factored in at quote stage.

Every kitchen is priced individually. Igor visits the property, assesses scope, materials, structural and conservation considerations, and provides a detailed written quotation itemised line by line. We do not publish indicative pricing online — the variables are too significant to give a useful range. Standard 20% VAT is shown separately on every quote.
Yes. Almost all of Knightsbridge sits within the Hans Town and Knightsbridge Conservation Areas, with a high concentration of listed buildings around Hans Place, Wilton Crescent and Cadogan Square. Internal kitchen refurbishment is normally permitted, but listed-building consent applies to any change affecting historic fabric — including chimney breasts, panelling, plasterwork and original windows. We work to those constraints by default.
Yes. Mansion blocks and prime apartments along Sloane Street, Brompton Road, Beauchamp Place and Pavilion Road are some of our most frequent SW1X and SW7 projects. We coordinate with managing agents on freeholder requirements, acoustic underlay, working hours, lift protection and shared-service approvals — all factored in at quote stage, not discovered after work begins.
Knightsbridge has some of the tightest access constraints in central London, particularly around Sloane Street, Brompton Road and the streets immediately around Harrods. We arrange contractor parking with the relevant council (RBKC for most addresses, City of Westminster for some east of Sloane Street), plan deliveries around residential and commercial restrictions, and factor lift-protection and porter coordination into the programme.
A complete bespoke kitchen refurbishment in Knightsbridge typically takes 6 to 10 weeks on site. Bespoke cabinetry and stone worktop lead times run in parallel during the design phase, so the on-site programme is not held up waiting for materials. Where structural alterations or listed-consent works are involved, the overall programme extends accordingly. We confirm exact timelines in writing after the property visit.
