Kitchens, In Richmond

Kitchens for Georgian Richmond,
the Hill, and the river.

Richmond’s housing stock is unusually distinctive. Georgian and early-Victorian terraces around Richmond Green, Edwardian houses climbing the Hill towards the Park, riverside properties along the Thames between Petersham and Richmond Bridge, and the village character of Ham further south. Each typology has its own constraints and the kitchen brief shifts accordingly.

The houses around Richmond Green and on the Hill are typically listed or sit within conservation areas, with original Georgian or Victorian detailing that the kitchen has to sit alongside without fighting. Riverside properties carry flood-zone considerations and access constraints that need to be factored into delivery and construction. Edwardian terraces on the streets running off Sheen Road and Kew Road often reward side-return or rear extensions opening to the garden.

We have refurbished kitchens across TW9 and TW10 long enough to know how London Borough of Richmond upon Thames handles planning, where listed-building consent is non-negotiable, and how to design for the specific brief each property typology calls for. Cabinetry is hand-built in our workshop. Worktops are templated on site, slab-matched at every joint. Installation is done by the carpenters who built the cabinetry.

Richmond Considerations

The detail that separates a kitchen
that belongs from one that doesn’t.

A bespoke kitchen in a Richmond Georgian terrace is a fundamentally different brief from a fitted kitchen elsewhere. Conservation status, listed-building consent, riverside flood-zone rules and the specific access constraints of Richmond’s narrow lanes all factor into how the project gets delivered.

We have refurbished kitchens across TW9 and TW10 long enough to know how the various Richmond Conservation Areas apply in practice, where listed-building consent is non-negotiable, how the flood-zone overlay affects riverside properties, and how London Borough of Richmond upon Thames handles parking permits and skip licences. All of it is factored in at quote stage.
