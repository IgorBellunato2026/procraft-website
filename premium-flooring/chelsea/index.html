Flooring, In Chelsea

A floor in a Chelsea house
has work to do.

Chelsea’s housing stock — Victorian and Georgian terraces, intimate mews properties, mansion blocks lining Cheyne Walk and the Embankment — was built around timber floors. The original boards have done their work for a hundred and fifty years. When they are replaced, what goes back has to belong in the room as much as the cornicing above it.

The most common Chelsea brief is the same in shape: lift the existing flooring across the ground floor, prepare the sub-floor properly, and install a hardwood floor that looks as if it was always there — herringbone or chevron in the principal rooms, wide-plank where the proportions allow, with the joints, edges and thresholds detailed by hand.

This is the work we do. We prepare the sub-floor without short-cuts. We acclimatise the timber on site before laying a single board. The pattern is set out from the geometry of the actual room, not the package on the box. The carpenters laying the floor are the same ones who measured it.

A Complete Service

From sub-floor up
to the final threshold.

Every flooring installation we deliver in Chelsea is end-to-end. One team. One point of contact. One standard. Sub-floor, acclimatisation, installation, finishing and snagging are all done by the same crew.

01

Survey & Specification

Measured survey, sub-floor moisture readings, acoustic build-up review and species selection appropriate to the room, the light and the rest of the interior.

02

Sub-Floor Preparation

Levelling, structural ply overlay where required, moisture barrier and acoustic underlay specified to mansion-block or freeholder requirements. The work that decides whether a floor lasts.

03

Pattern Set-Out

Chevron, herringbone, single-direction parquet and wide-plank set out from the actual room geometry — not the doorway, not the longest wall, but where the eye reads it correctly.

04

Hardwood & Engineered

Solid oak, walnut, smoked oak, prime grade engineered boards. Sourced from established UK and European suppliers, acclimatised on site for 5 to 7 days before installation begins.

05

Hand Finishing

Borders, thresholds, scotia, bespoke transition strips and skirting reinstatement, finished by hand to a level you cannot reproduce with off-the-shelf trim packs.

06

Sealing & Snagging

Hardwax-oil or lacquer finish applied to specification, with full protection of adjacent rooms and skirting. Final walk-through and snag list signed off personally before handover.

Chelsea Considerations

The detail that decides
whether a floor lasts.

A premium hardwood floor in a Chelsea Victorian terrace is not the same brief as the same floor in a new-build apartment. The variables that matter are different: original joists, suspended timber sub-floors, residual moisture, mansion-block acoustic specifications, the threshold detail at the door of an original cornicing room.

We have laid floors across Chelsea for long enough to know what those variables are, where they cause failure if ignored, and how to design around them. Sub-floor preparation, acclimatisation timing, pattern set-out and freeholder approvals are all factored in at quote stage, not discovered mid-project.
