Flooring, In Richmond

A floor in a Richmond house
has work to do.

Richmond’s housing stock is unusually varied: Georgian and Victorian terraces around Richmond Green, Edwardian houses climbing Richmond Hill, riverside properties along the Thames, and the listed buildings around Petersham and Ham. The original boards have done their work for a hundred years and more. When they are replaced, what goes back has to belong in the room.

The Richmond brief tends to be honest and practical: lift the existing flooring across the principal rooms, prepare the sub-floor properly — with extra attention to moisture management on riverside addresses — and install a hardwood floor that suits the building. Herringbone or chevron in formal rooms, wide-plank engineered oak in living and family spaces.

This is the work we do. We prepare the sub-floor without short-cuts. We acclimatise the timber on site before laying a single board. The pattern is set out from the geometry of the actual room. The carpenters laying the floor are the same ones who measured it.

A Complete Service

From sub-floor up
to the final threshold.

Every flooring installation we deliver in Richmond is end-to-end. One team. One point of contact. One standard.

01

Survey & Specification

Measured survey, sub-floor moisture readings, acoustic build-up review and species selection appropriate to the room, the light and the rest of the interior.

02

Sub-Floor Preparation

Levelling, structural ply overlay where required, moisture barrier and acoustic underlay specified to mansion-block or freeholder requirements.

03

Pattern Set-Out

Chevron, herringbone, single-direction parquet and wide-plank set out from the actual room geometry — not the doorway, not the longest wall.

04

Hardwood & Engineered

Solid oak, walnut, smoked oak, prime grade engineered boards. Sourced from established UK and European suppliers, acclimatised on site for 5 to 7 days.

05

Hand Finishing

Borders, thresholds, scotia, bespoke transition strips and skirting reinstatement, finished by hand to a level you cannot reproduce with off-the-shelf trim packs.

06

Sealing & Snagging

Hardwax-oil or lacquer finish applied to specification, with full protection of adjacent rooms and skirting. Final walk-through and snag list signed off personally before handover.

Richmond Considerations

The detail that decides
whether a floor lasts.

A premium hardwood floor in a Richmond Georgian terrace is not the same brief as the same floor in a new-build apartment. The variables that matter are different: original joists, suspended timber sub-floors, residual moisture from below, riverside flood-zone considerations, conservation-area constraints around Richmond Green and Petersham.

We have laid floors across TW9 and TW10 long enough to know what those variables are, where they cause failure if ignored, and how to design around them. Sub-floor preparation, acclimatisation timing, pattern set-out and freeholder approvals are all factored in at quote stage, not discovered mid-project.
