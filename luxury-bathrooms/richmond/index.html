Bathrooms, In Richmond

Bathrooms designed for Georgian Richmond,
the Hill, and the river.

Richmond’s housing stock is unusually distinctive. Georgian and early-Victorian terraces around Richmond Green, Edwardian houses climbing the Hill towards the Park, riverside properties along the Thames, and the village character of Petersham and Ham. Each typology calls for a slightly different bathroom brief.

The technical demand is consistent across all of them. Tanking to BS 8102, acoustic underlay specified to managing-agent rules where applicable, listed-building consent where original fabric is touched, and flood-zone considerations on riverside addresses. The aesthetic brief shifts with the building — a Georgian house on Richmond Green calls for different detailing than a riverside Edwardian villa or a mews-style cottage in Ham.

We deliver both halves. The visible work is finished to hotel standard at residential scale; the underlying construction is built to the technical specification the building actually requires.

Richmond Considerations

Where Richmond bathrooms
most often go wrong.

The bathrooms that fail in Richmond fail in predictable ways. Tanking cut short on a Hill house, leading to a leak through the principal reception room below. Listed-building consent overlooked on a Georgian terrace around Richmond Green. Flood-zone considerations missed on a riverside property. Stone laid without proper substrate prep so it cracks in the first heating season.

None of these are inevitable. We have refurbished bathrooms across TW9 and TW10 long enough to know which corners can be cut and which cannot — and we never cut the latter.
