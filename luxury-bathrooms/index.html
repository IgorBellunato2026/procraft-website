Why It Matters

A bathroom that fails
is a renovation that fails.

The two things that ruin a bathroom renovation are bad waterproofing and rushed tiling. Both are invisible at handover. Both surface a year or two later — at exactly the moment the work was supposed to start paying off.

ProCraft builds bathrooms slowly and properly. Tanking systems are specified to the substrate, not chosen for convenience. Tiles are dry-laid before they are fixed, so the layout sits exactly where the eye expects it. Wastes, traps and brassware are pressure-tested before tiles go on. Grout is cured before the room is handed over. None of this is glamorous. All of it is the difference between a bathroom that lasts a year and a bathroom that lasts twenty.

And the visible work — the wall of marble behind a freestanding bath, the herringbone field running unbroken across a wet zone, the brushed gold mixer that lines up perfectly with the centre of the basin — is the result of weeks of decisions made carefully, not days of decisions made on site.

Layout, elevations, materials and brassware schedule produced before any work begins. You approve the scheme on paper, not as a series of on-site decisions.

06

Premium brassware installed, bath and shower tested, sanitaryware fitted, mirrors and lighting hung. Every fixture aligned to the millimetre before sign-off.

Materials & Finishes

The materials we work with —
and how we install them.

A bathroom is judged on its details. These are the materials, brands and details that consistently produce the bathrooms we are proud to put our name to.

Wall & floor tile
Marble (Carrara, Calacatta, Statuario), porcelain large-format slabs (Italian and Spanish manufacture), ceramic herringbone and brick bond, mosaic and Zellige. Layouts dry-laid before fixing, with cuts planned at the design stage so no awkward slivers appear at edges.
Waterproofing
Schlüter, Mapei or Ardex tanking systems specified to substrate. Wet zones fully tanked floor-to-ceiling. Joints and corners reinforced with bandage. Certified prior to tiling.
Brassware
Brushed brass, brushed gold, polished chrome, brushed steel, matte black. Sourced from Crosswater, Perrin & Rowe, Drummonds, Lefroy Brooks, Vola and Hansgrohe. Mixer, shower head and waste co-ordinated as a single suite.
Sanitaryware
Wall-hung WCs from Geberit (concealed cisterns), Duravit, Catalano and Villeroy & Boch. Vanity basins from Lusso Stone, Burlington, Drummonds and bespoke marble or stone basins.
Showers & baths
Walk-in (curbless) showers with linear drainage, low-iron glass screens, recessed niches. Freestanding baths from BC Designs, Victoria + Albert and Drummonds; built-in baths panelled in stone or tile.
Underfloor heating
Warmup and ProWarm electric mat or loose-wire systems with 6mm insulation boards. Programmable thermostats on a dedicated 16A circuit. Installed beneath the tile bed prior to grouting.
Lighting & mirrors
IP-rated recessed downlights, LED mirror lighting (anti-fog), task lighting around the vanity, accent lighting in niches. All on dimmer circuits where the brief calls for it.
