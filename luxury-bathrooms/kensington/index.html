Bathrooms, In Kensington

Bathrooms designed for the
scale of a Kensington house.

Kensington’s housing stock has more room to play with than most parts of London. Tall stucco-fronted terraces around Edwardes Square and Holland Park, generous mansion-block flats along Cornwall Gardens and Queen’s Gate, and garden-square houses with bathrooms that can run to the size of a normal-sized bedroom. The brief almost always benefits from that scale — freestanding baths read in proportion, walk-in showers can be properly walk-in, and twin vanities work without compromise.

The technical brief is the same as anywhere in central London: tanking to BS 8102, acoustic underlay specified to managing-agent rules, listed-building consent where original fabric is touched, and freeholder approvals coordinated cleanly. The difference in Kensington is that the available room and the calibre of the original architecture both raise expectations on what the finished bathroom should look like.

We deliver both. The visible work is finished to hotel standard at residential scale; the underlying construction is built to last the life of the property.

Kensington Considerations

Where Kensington bathrooms
most often go wrong.

The bathrooms that fail in Kensington fail in predictable ways. Inadequate tanking that allows a leak into the flat below. Listed-building consent breached on original cornicing or panelling. Acoustic standards missed because nobody asked the managing agent. Stone laid without proper substrate prep so it cracks in the first heating season.

None of these are inevitable. Each is a function of cutting the wrong corner, by a contractor who does not understand the building. We have refurbished bathrooms across W8 and SW7 long enough to know which corners can be cut and which cannot — and we never cut the latter.
