Bathrooms, In Notting Hill

Bathrooms designed for the
Ladbroke Estate and the streets around it.

Notting Hill’s housing stock is unusually consistent. The pastel-fronted Victorian terraces around the Ladbroke Estate are typically four storeys, with bathrooms positioned on the upper floors above bedrooms and reception rooms. The technical demand on tanking and acoustic specification is high — a leak through three storeys of period plasterwork is the single most expensive failure mode in any W11 refurbishment.

The aesthetic brief is equally demanding. The original detailing on these houses — cornicing, sash windows, panelling, marble fireplaces — sets the standard for what a contemporary bathroom can sit alongside. The finished suite has to feel like it has always belonged in the building.

We deliver both halves. The visible work is finished to hotel standard at residential scale; the underlying construction is built to the technical specification the building actually requires — full BS 8102 tanking, acoustic underlay specified to lease and freeholder requirements, listed-building consent coordinated where the original fabric is touched.

Notting Hill Considerations

Where Notting Hill bathrooms
most often go wrong.

The bathrooms that fail in Notting Hill fail in predictable ways. Tanking cut short to keep a programme on track, leading to a leak through three storeys of period plasterwork below. Listed-building consent overlooked on a Ladbroke Estate terrace. Garden-square committee approvals forgotten until skip placement is challenged. Stone laid without proper substrate prep so it cracks in the first heating season.

None of these are inevitable. We have refurbished bathrooms across W11 long enough to know which corners can be cut and which cannot — and we never cut the latter.
