Bathrooms, In Fulham

Bathrooms designed for Fulham’s
Victorian terraces and mansion blocks.

Fulham’s housing stock is unusually consistent. Long rows of Victorian terraces around Parsons Green, Hurlingham and Munster Village; period mansion blocks along New Kings Road and Fulham Road. Bathrooms in the terraces are typically positioned on the upper floors above bedrooms and reception rooms; bathrooms in the mansion blocks almost always sit above other dwellings entirely.

The technical demand is high in both. Tanking to BS 8102 is non-negotiable. Acoustic underlay specifications need to satisfy managing-agent rules in the mansion blocks. Listed-building consent applies in the small number of listed properties around the conservation areas. Side-return extensions to create larger ground-floor bathrooms are an increasingly common Fulham brief.

We deliver both halves. The visible work is finished to hotel standard at residential scale; the underlying construction is built to the technical specification the building actually requires.

Fulham Considerations

Where Fulham bathrooms
most often go wrong.

The bathrooms that fail in Fulham fail in predictable ways. Tanking cut short on a terrace, leading to a leak through the principal bedroom below. Acoustic specs missed in a mansion-block flat because nobody read the lease. Conservation status overlooked on a Hurlingham property. Stone laid without proper substrate prep so it cracks in the first heating season.

None of these are inevitable. We have refurbished bathrooms across SW6 long enough to know which corners can be cut and which cannot — and we never cut the latter.
