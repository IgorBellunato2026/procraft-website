Bathrooms, In Hampstead

Bathrooms designed for one of
London’s most varied villages.

Hampstead’s housing stock varies more than most of London. Georgian and Victorian village houses around Hampstead Village proper, Arts and Crafts homes around Frognal and Redington Road with hand-cut joinery and exposed timber, tall Belsize Park terraces with bathrooms tucked under gables, and Edwardian mansion blocks along Fitzjohn’s Avenue with managing-agent rules to satisfy. Every property type wants a slightly different bathroom brief.

The technical brief is the same as anywhere in London: tanking to BS 8102, acoustic underlay specified to managing-agent rules where applicable, listed-building consent where original fabric is touched, and access planned around the village reality — the steep hill gradients, the narrow lanes, the parking constraints — rather than against it.

We deliver both halves. The visible work is finished to hotel standard at residential scale; the underlying construction is built to the standard the building actually requires.

Hampstead Considerations

Where Hampstead bathrooms
most often go wrong.

The bathrooms that fail in Hampstead fail in predictable ways. Tanking cut short to keep a programme on track, leading to a leak into the principal living room below. Listed-building consent overlooked on a Village house. Access planning underestimated, so deliveries miss the parking window and the programme slips. Stone laid without proper substrate prep so it cracks in the first heating season.

None of these are inevitable. We have refurbished bathrooms across NW3 long enough to know which corners can be cut and which cannot — and we never cut the latter.

Yes. The Hampstead Conservation Area covers Hampstead Village and much of NW3, with a high concentration of listed buildings around Church Row, Holly Hill and Flask Walk. Internal bathroom alterations to non-historic fabric are usually permitted, but listed-building consent applies where original detail (panelling, plasterwork, joinery, original tiling) is affected. We coordinate consent applications through our planning network when required.
Hampstead Village has tight access constraints — narrow lanes, steep hill gradients around Heath Street and Holly Hill, single-vehicle frontages and limited parking. We arrange contractor parking with London Borough of Camden ahead of every project, plan deliveries around the access reality, and factor lift-and-carry constraints into the programme rather than discovering them mid-project.
