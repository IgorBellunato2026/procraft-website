Painting & Decorating, In Notting Hill

A finish in a Notting Hill house
is held to a different standard.

Notting Hill’s housing stock is unusually photogenic and unusually consistent: pastel-fronted Victorian terraces around the Ladbroke and Norland estates, garden-square houses opening onto communal gardens, listed townhouses on the principal streets. Most projects relate to the rear opening of the ground floor and a careful relationship with the communal garden behind.

The Notting Hill brief is almost always the same in shape: Strip back to sound surfaces, premium paint specification, hand-cut detail throughout, with stucco-front exterior restoration in pastel finishes appropriate to the conservation area.

This is the work we do. We prepare surfaces properly, prime correctly, and finish by hand to a level you cannot reproduce with a roller. The painters are trusted members of our crew, not a sub-contracted team brought in for the day.

Notting Hill Considerations

The detail that separates a finish
that lasts from one that doesn’t.

A decoration in a Notting Hill listed Victorian terrace is a fundamentally different brief from a decoration elsewhere. The constraints are different, the consent regime is layered (planning, listed-building consent, garden-committee approvals), and the relationship to the communal garden at the rear is the design move that has to work.

We have refurbished decoration projects across W11 long enough to know how those layered approvals operate in practice, how the major freeholders structure consent, and how to deliver into addresses where rear extensions touch communal gardens. All of it is factored in at quote stage.
