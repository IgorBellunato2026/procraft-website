Why It Matters

The finish you can see is
the preparation you cannot.

Most people who are unhappy with a decorator's work were not let down by the paint — they were let down by the prep. Holes filled and not sanded flat. Caulk applied so heavily it telegraphs through the topcoat. Walls painted before they were properly cleaned, primed and stabilised. Within six months, every short-cut shows.

ProCraft does not work this way. Every wall is filled, sanded and re-sanded until it reads flat under raked light. Every join between joinery and wall is cut in by hand, not flooded with caulk. Every surface is primed with the right system for the substrate — alkali-resistant for new plaster, stain-block for water marks, bonding primer for previously oil-painted timber.

Only then does the topcoat go on. Two coats minimum, brush-laid or roller-rolled depending on the surface, with the finish carefully chosen for the room. The result is a redecoration that still looks crisp three years later — which is the only test of decorating that matters.

Specification

The decisions that decide
how the room ages.

Decoration is the sum of dozens of small specification decisions. These are the ones that matter — and the way ProCraft approaches each one.

Surface preparation
All holes filled with the correct filler (lightweight for shallow defects, two-pack for structural), sanded back to flat, dust-extracted and wiped clean before any paint is opened. Cracks raked out and bridged with reinforcing tape where necessary.
Priming system
Alkali-resistant primer on new or skim-coated plaster. Stain-block on water marks, nicotine and timber knots. Bonding primer on previously oil-painted woodwork. Proper primers, applied properly, are what stops a topcoat failing in 18 months.
Paint brand and system
Farrow & Ball, Little Greene, Paint & Paper Library and Edward Bulmer for premium interiors. Mylands for specialist colours. Tikkurila and Caparol for high-traffic and exteriors. Always two coats of finish minimum.
Cutting in and lines
Cut-in lines between walls and ceilings, walls and joinery, walls and skirtings are hand-brushed by an experienced decorator — not relied on tape. Tape is used to protect, not to define the line.
Protection of property
Floors covered with breathable cotton dust sheets over hardboard or correx where required. Furniture moved, bagged or covered in poly. Ironmongery removed and bagged. Carpets edge-protected before any cutting in.
Site discipline
The site is cleaned down at the end of every working day. Tools washed, bins emptied, dust sheets re-laid. The property remains liveable throughout the work where possible.
Aftercare
A small touch-up tin of every colour used is left with the client at handover, labelled by room and surface. Snags resolved free of charge in the first 12 months.
