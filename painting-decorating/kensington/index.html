Painting & Decorating, In Kensington

A finish in a Kensington house
is held to a different standard.

The housing stock between Kensington High Street and Holland Park — stucco-fronted Victorian and Edwardian terraces, Edwardian mansion blocks, garden-square houses around Kensington Square and Edwardes Square — was built before electricity reached it. The decoration brief here lives or dies on respecting that fabric.

The Kensington brief is almost always the same in shape: Strip back to sound surfaces, lining paper where the plaster demands it, finish in premium paints, with stucco exterior restoration and sash-window refurbishment as part of the same crew.

This is the work we do. We prepare surfaces properly, prime correctly, and finish by hand to a level you cannot reproduce with a roller. The painters are trusted members of our crew, not a sub-contracted team brought in for the day.

Every painting and decorating project we deliver in Kensington is end-to-end. One team. One point of contact. One standard.

01

Surface Preparation

Fillers, sanding, prep coats, mould treatment, lining paper where needed. The work that decides whether a finish lasts five years or fifteen.

02

Premium Paint Specification

Farrow & Ball, Little Greene, Paint & Paper Library, Edward Bulmer. Specified to suit the room’s light, the finish you want, and the rest of the house.

Hand-Cut Edges

Cut-in by hand, not masked. Crisp lines at cornicing, skirting, panelling and door frames. The visible quality difference.

Sash window restoration, panelling repair, door refurbishment, hand-stripping where the building demands it.

Exterior Decorating

Stucco, render, sash window exteriors and front doors, prepared and finished to specification with appropriate masonry, wood and metal paints.

06

Snagging & Handover

Walk-through with you before sign-off. Touch-ups handled on the spot, no return visits needed.

Kensington Considerations

The detail that separates a finish
that lasts from one that doesn’t.

A decoration in a Kensington stucco-fronted terrace is not the same brief as a decoration in a new-build apartment. The variables that matter are different, the constraints are different, and the design moves that work elsewhere fail here.

We have refurbished decoration projects across W8 and SW7 long enough to know what those variables are: where the load-bearing walls live, how Royal Borough of Kensington and Chelsea conservation areas affect external work, how mansion-block managing agents handle freeholder approvals, and where access genuinely constrains delivery. All of it is factored in at quote stage, not discovered mid-project.
