The Project

Full bathroom
refurbishment, London.

A complete bathroom refurbishment delivered end-to-end for a London property. The brief was bold rather than neutral. A freestanding cast-iron roll-top bath on clawfoot, finished in coral, sits centred in front of a white multi-pane window with frosted glazing for privacy. A geometric black-and-white star-pattern tile floor — encaustic-style, laid by hand — anchors the whole room. Walls in a deep black tone hold everything together; the white ceiling lifts the proportions; a brass drum pendant sits warm against the dark.

The walk-in shower sits behind a brass-framed reeded-glass screen, lined inside in coral hexagonal tile that picks up the colour of the bath. The freestanding bath tap and shower brassware are brushed brass throughout, including the brass detailing on the shower door frame and recessed inset. Opposite the shower, a deep-green Shaker-style vanity with a single-piece white basin and brushed brass tap completes the composition. The work involved stripping the bathroom back to brick, tanking the wet area to BS 8102 standard, rerouting plumbing to allow the freestanding bath to sit centrally, and a full re-board for acoustic compliance with the building.

The room reads as a single composed interior — confident, characterful, with the kind of personality a catalogue specification cannot deliver. Designed and built by the ProCraft crew, signed off personally on completion. The kind of bathroom that rewards a brief that knows what it wants.
