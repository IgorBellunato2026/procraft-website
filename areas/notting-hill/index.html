Notting Hill, In Practice

Refurbishment for Notting Hill homes,
by craftsmen who know them.

Notting Hill is one of the most architecturally distinctive parts of London — pastel-fronted Victorian terraces fronting the communal garden squares of the Ladbroke Estate, more austere terraces around Westbourne Grove, and a rich supply of period flat conversions. The character is unusually consistent, and the conservation framework that protects it is correspondingly strict.

Our Notting Hill projects typically combine sympathetic restoration of original features — cornicing, panelling, sash windows, marble fireplaces, original boards — with a contemporary insertion: a hand-built kitchen, a marble bathroom suite, a side-return or rear extension designed to satisfy both the building and the planning officer. The standard is set by the building: anything we add must read as if it has always been there.

We work routinely within the Ladbroke and Norland Conservation Areas and on listed buildings under Royal Borough of Kensington and Chelsea. Planning, party-wall awareness, garden-committee liaison and access constraints are factored in at quote stage — not discovered mid-project.
