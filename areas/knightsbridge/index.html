Knightsbridge, In Practice

Refurbishment for Knightsbridge homes,
by craftsmen who know them.

Knightsbridge is one of the most tightly held parts of central London — listed townhouses around Hans Place and Wilton Crescent, mansion blocks along Sloane Street and Brompton Road, and a quiet supply of high-spec apartments behind Beauchamp Place. Each typology brings its own complications: managing agents to brief, porters to coordinate, lifts to protect, and conservation officers who notice everything.

Our Knightsbridge projects typically combine sympathetic restoration of original features — cornicing, panelling, sash windows, fireplaces — with a contemporary insertion: a hand-built kitchen, a marble bathroom suite, joinery integrated into the architecture rather than imposed on it. The standard is set by the building: anything we add must read as if it has always been there.

We work routinely on listed buildings and within the Hans Town and Knightsbridge conservation areas. Planning, party-wall awareness, porter coordination, lift access and out-of-hours working windows are factored in at quote stage — not discovered mid-project.
