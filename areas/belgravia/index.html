Belgravia, In Practice

Refurbishment for Belgravia homes,
by craftsmen who know them.

Belgravia is the most architecturally formal residential quarter in London — long, uninterrupted runs of white-stucco Regency terraces around Belgrave Square, Eaton Square and Chester Square, with intimate mews tucked behind every principal street. Almost the entire neighbourhood is listed, and almost every property sits under the Grosvenor Estate. The work has to satisfy the building, the estate and the conservation officer in equal measure.

Our Belgravia projects typically combine sympathetic restoration of original features — cornicing, panelling, sash windows, marble fireplaces, original ironwork — with a contemporary insertion: a hand-built kitchen, a marble bathroom suite, joinery integrated into the architecture rather than imposed on it. The standard is set by the building: anything we add must read as if it has always been there.

We work routinely on listed buildings within the Belgravia Conservation Area. Estate consents, planning, party-wall awareness, porter coordination, lift access and out-of-hours working windows are factored in at quote stage — not discovered mid-project.
