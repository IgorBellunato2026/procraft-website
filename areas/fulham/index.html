Fulham, In Practice

Refurbishment for Fulham homes,
by craftsmen who know them.

Fulham is one of the great upsizing neighbourhoods of South West London — long runs of Victorian terraces around Parsons Green and the Hurlingham area, semi-detached family houses on the quieter streets, and period mansion blocks along the New Kings Road. The architecture is unusually consistent, and the briefs are equally consistent: a side-return extension, a remodelled kitchen, a properly thought-through master suite, a lower-ground floor that finally works.

Our Fulham projects typically combine sympathetic restoration of original features — cornicing, panelling, sash windows, original fireplaces — with a contemporary insertion: a hand-built kitchen, a marble bathroom, a side-return that opens up the back of the house, or a lateral conversion across two flats. The standard is set by the building: anything we add must read as if it has always been there.

We work routinely within Fulham Conservation Areas under London Borough of Hammersmith and Fulham. Planning, party-wall awareness, neighbour notifications and access constraints are factored in at quote stage — not discovered mid-project.
