Hampstead, In Practice

Refurbishment for Hampstead homes,
by craftsmen who know them.

Hampstead is one of the few parts of London where the village character has genuinely survived — Georgian houses around Church Row and Flask Walk, Victorian streets climbing up to the Heath, Arts and Crafts homes on the quieter lanes, and Edwardian mansion blocks down towards Belsize Park. The architecture varies; the standard expected of the work does not.

Our Hampstead projects typically combine sympathetic restoration of original features — cornicing, panelling, sash windows, fireplaces, original boards — with a contemporary insertion: a hand-built kitchen, a marble bathroom, a structural opening at the rear or a remodelled lower-ground floor. The standard is set by the building: anything we add must read as if it has always been there.

We work routinely within the Hampstead Conservation Area and on listed buildings under London Borough of Camden. Planning, permits, party-wall awareness, narrow-lane access and scaffolding logistics are factored in at quote stage — not discovered mid-project.
